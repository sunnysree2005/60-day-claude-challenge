Current Strengths
Microcontroller implementation (Arduino environment).

Fundamental analog & digital communication theory (AM, FM, Filter Design).

Data structures execution (Stacks, Queues, Linked Lists) in C++.

Analytical signal processing modeling in MATLAB.

Missing Skills (Areas to Grow)

Low-level protocols deeper exposure (I2C, SPI, UART registers configuration).

Recommended Learning Roadmap
Month 1: Transition from basic Arduino wrappers to native C programming for microcontrollers (AVR or STM32) and master direct register manipulation.

Month 2: Learn multi-layer PCB design principles using KiCAD or Altium Designer, converting standalone prototypes into production-grade custom boards.

Month 3: Implement basic FreeRTOS tasks to understand multitasking in embedded environments; integrate fundamental Machine Learning concepts on Edge devices (TinyML).

SECTION 12: Interview Talking Points
The ElectroKnights Robotic Arm: Be ready to talk about the 4-DOF mini robotic arm. Focus on the integration of Arduino Nano with the MG90S servos, how you managed power constraints, and how the team collaborated under the workshop framework.

Algorithmic C++ Implementation: Highlight your knowledge of linked lists and tree structures. Explain how strong software principles make hardware execution more memory-efficient and faster.

Filter Design Engineering: Be prepared to discuss your filter design calculations (LPF, HPF, BPF). Recruiters love candidates who can explain why a specific component or cut-off frequency was selected during mathematical modeling.
