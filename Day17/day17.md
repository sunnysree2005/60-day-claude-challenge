# Day 17: Vehicle Cost Analysis Dashboard (Royal Enfield Classic 350)

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on building an interactive, responsive **Vehicle Cost Analysis Dashboard** using Claude's generation capabilities and a vehicle dataset. The dashboard systematically evaluates the operational economics, periodic maintenance expenses, fuel efficiency, and environmental footprints of a **Royal Enfield Classic 350 (349cc)** motorcycle over a standard analytical timeline.

---

## 🛠️ 1. Vehicle Configuration & Profile
*   **Vehicle Model:** Royal Enfield Classic 350 (J-Series Engine)
*   **Engine Capacity:** 349cc
*   **Fuel Type:** Petrol / Octane
*   **Average Efficiency (Mileage):** 35 km/L
*   **Estimated Annual Running:** 10,000 km
*   **Service Interval:** 5,000 km (2 Periodic Services per Year)

---

## 🔍 2. Key Analysis Findings

Based on the dataset processing and the calculated metrics within the generated HTML engine, here is the economic profile of running the Royal Enfield Classic 350 annually:

1.  **Fuel Consumption & Cost Dominance:** To cover the annual target of 10,000 km with a mileage of 35 km/L, the vehicle requires approximately **285.7 Liters** of petrol. This makes fuel the largest recurring operational cost, accounting for nearly 70% of the total annual running budget.
2.  **Maintenance Viability:** The J-series 349cc engine relies on specific high-viscosity lubricants (15W50 grade). While periodic servicing every 5,000 km keeps the engine healthy and predictable, general upkeep expenses remain stable compared to high-end multi-cylinder sportbikes, making it a sustainable choice for long-term ownership.
3.  **Cost Per Kilometer Efficiency:** Despite being a heavier 350cc machine, the net running cost per kilometer stays highly competitive due to its optimized fuel delivery system, balancing commuter practicalities with cruiser performance.
4.  **Carbon Footprint Tracking:** Adhering to an estimated emission standard of roughly 70g of $CO_2$ per km for mid-displacement single-cylinder engines, traveling 10,000 km outputs around **700 kg of $CO_2$** annually. This underscores the impact of timely spark plug changes and clean air filter replacements on overall environmental efficiency.
