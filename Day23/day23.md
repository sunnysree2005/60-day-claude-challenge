

# Customer & MVP Blueprint

## Executive Summary

**RoboTeach BD** is a targeted EdTech and hardware-kit startup aiming to democratize STEM and Robotics education for school and college students in Bangladesh. By stripping away over-engineered components and focusing on local, curriculum-aligned, and affordable hardware kits paired with native Bangla video mentorship, the startup aims to bridge the gap between theoretical science and hands-on engineering innovation.

---

| Parameter | Description |
| --- | --- |

| **Psychographics** | Tech-conscious, future-oriented, values hands-on practical skills over rote memorization. |
| **Behaviors** | Regularly invests in coaching, extra-curricular tech workshops, and purchasing educational toys online. |

## Buyer Persona

> **Name:** Tanvir Ahmed (42, Tech-Conscious Parent)
> **Role:** Bank Manager & Parent of a 14-year-old high schooler in Dhaka.
> **Goals:** Wants his child to learn future-ready skills (coding/robotics) and reduce screen time spent on video games.
> **Pain Points:** Finds existing international robotics kits (like LEGO Mindstorms) prohibitively expensive ($150+), and local components lack structured, beginner-friendly tutorials in Bangla.

---

## Top 10 Customer Pain Points

1. High cost of imported robotics and micro-controller kits.
2. Lack of structured learning paths for beginners.
3. Language barrier (most quality documentation and tutorials are strictly in English).
4. Sourcing components reliably from scattered local markets (e.g., Nawabpur/Dhaka).
5. Defective or cloned components without any replacement warranty.
6. Parents' anxiety regarding productive screen-time for children.
7. Lack of guidance/mentorship when code or circuits fail to debug.
8. School curriculums remain overly theoretical without lab access.
9. High cost of physical engineering/robotics workshops in major cities.
10. Fear of damaging sensitive electronics due to wrong wiring.

## Customer Journey

```
Awareness ──> Consideration ──> Purchase ──> Retention

```

* **Awareness:** Parent/Student sees a short Facebook video of a 12-year-old building a Line Follower Robot using a RoboTeach BD kit.
* **Consideration:** Reads reviews on the website, downloads a free sample project guide in Bangla, and compares pricing with imported alternatives.
* **Purchase:** Buys the "RoboStart Kit" (Starter Kit) via bKash with a 6-month component replacement warranty.
* **Retention:** Completes the built-in projects, joins the exclusive Discord/Facebook support community, and upgrades to the advanced "IoT & Automation Kit".

---

## Key Customer Objections

* **"Is it too difficult for my child?"** *Counter:* The kit comes with plug-and-play jumper wires (no soldering required) and step-by-step cartoon-illustrated video guides.
* **"What if a component breaks?"** *Counter:* We offer an industry-first 3-month immediate replacement warranty on microcontrollers and sensors.

---

## Key Buying Triggers

* **School Science/Robotics Fairs:** Rapid increase in demand right before local school/college competitions.
* **Exam Breaks:** Parents looking for productive engagement activities during post-exam vacations.

---

## MVP Recommendation

* **What should be built first?** A single "Basic Robotics Kit" comprising an Arduino Uno clone, basic sensors (IR, Ultrasonic), a chassis, and access to a restricted YouTube playlist containing 10 structured lessons in Bangla.
* **What should NOT be built?** Do *not* build a custom mobile app, a dedicated web LMS platform, or complex multi-functional robotic arm kits initially.
* **Success Metrics:** 15% month-on-month growth in kit sales, >80% project completion rate from early buyers, and positive community reviews.

---

## MoSCoW Prioritization

### Must Have

* Plug-and-play component hardware pack (No-soldering required).
* 10 core video tutorials hosted securely on a private YouTube playlist.
* QR-code linked PDF manual written completely in Bangla.
* Dedicated WhatsApp/Facebook Group support line for debugging.

### Should Have

* Dedicated webpage with a simple checkout flow and integrated bKash/Nagad payment options.
* A 3-month replacement warranty on core microcontrollers.

### Could Have

* Gamified learning badges or certificates of completion printable online.
* Cash on Delivery option outside of Dhaka.

### Won't Have

* Native Android/iOS App.
* Advanced AI/Machine Learning components in the basic starter kit.

---

## Top 5 Risks

1. **Supply Chain Disruption:** Dependency on importing raw components from China.
2. **Content Piracy:** Video courses being leaked on public torrents/Facebook pages.
3. **Hardware Failure Rates:** Cloned microcontrollers burning out due to user mishandling.
4. **Market Education Gap:** Parents mistaking it for a complex academic burden rather than a fun skill.
5. **Short Customer Lifecycle:** Students outgrowing the basic kit within a few months.
